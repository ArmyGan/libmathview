Try GtkMathView online!

This page allows you to try GtkMathView online. You can paste a well-formed fragment of MathML in the form below, the MathML will be converted into SVG, the SVG will then be rendered into a PNG image, which you will get as output.

Fill in the form below with the MathML fragment you want to render, then click the Render button to get the corresponding PNG image.

WARNINGS

  1. Any submitted MathML code may be archived for collecting statistics and verifying GtkMathView's rendering.
  2. The rsvg utility that renders SVG into PNG may cut off parts of the formula that are close to its boundaries.

TIPS

  1. Do not forget to use the MathML namespace for every MathML element you want to be rendered. The simplest thing to do is to declare a default namespace as in the example below.
  2. Only MathML presentation tags are understood by GtkMathView.

You are welcome to notify ugly or broken renderings using the bugzilla module for GtkMathView.
